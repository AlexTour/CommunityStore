Community Store

The RURBANIVE Community Store connects people, practices, and knowledge to support rural–urban collaboration.

What Is the Community Store?

The RURBANIVE Community Store supports collaboration between rural and urban actors by providing access to tools, shared spaces for engagement, immersive experiences, and reusable knowledge assets.

All resources are grounded in practice and designed to be applied, adapted, and extended across different territorial contexts.

Rural–Urban Enablers (RUEs)

A structured catalogue of tools and services addressing concrete rural–urban challenges.

  • What the tool or service does
  • Who it is intended for
  • How it was used within the project
  • What type of outputs it produces

RUEs can be browsed by thematic domain and territorial applicability.

Immersive Campaigns

A collection of immersive content developed through rural–urban co-creation activities.

Users can explore immersive material directly through a web browser, without specialised hardware, to understand the contexts, narratives, and experiences developed during the project.

Knowledge Inventory

A curated repository of project knowledge, supporting learning, reuse, and comparison across rural–urban contexts.

  • Good practices from Rural–Urban Co-Creation Labs (RUCLs)
  • Practice abstracts and business models
  • Policy briefs and synthesis documents

Community of Practice Suite

An integrated entry point to participatory processes and stakeholder interaction tools, based on the Decidim platform.

The suite supports dialogue, engagement, and collective reflection among rural and urban actors involved in rural–urban development processes.

Access and Availability

The Community Store is currently available in English. Additional languages are provided where content has been developed and used in specific RUCL localities. Access policies and integration levels vary by section and tool maturity.
